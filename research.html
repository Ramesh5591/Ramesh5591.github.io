Research Statement

Bridging computational biology and artificial intelligence to advance plant science

My research interests lie at the dynamic intersection of plant genomics, computational biology, and artificial intelligence. I am driven by the conviction that understanding the molecular complexity of plant systems requires innovative computational approaches that can extract meaningful patterns from vast genomic datasets and translate these insights into actionable knowledge for crop improvement and sustainable agriculture.

Research Philosophy

The grand challenges facing global agriculture‚Äîclimate change, emerging plant pathogens, and food security‚Äîdemand interdisciplinary solutions that leverage cutting-edge computational methods. My work is founded on the principle that by integrating genome-wide analyses with machine learning and deep learning models, we can uncover the fundamental mechanisms governing plant defense and stress responses, ultimately accelerating the development of resilient crop varieties.

Current Research Focus

Plant Defense Mechanisms

Investigating the molecular architecture of plant immunity through genome-wide characterization of resistance gene families (NLRs) and transcription factors (NAC, MYB), focusing on pattern-triggered immunity (PTI) and effector-triggered immunity (ETI) pathways.

Computational Genomics

Employing advanced bioinformatics pipelines to conduct comprehensive analyses of gene families, including phylogenetic reconstruction, synteny mapping, gene duplication patterns, and cis-regulatory element identification across diverse plant species.

AI-Driven Prediction

Developing deep learning architectures for predicting gene function, protein-protein interactions, and stress response pathways. My models incorporate multi-layer feature engineering from sequence, structure, and expression data to achieve robust predictions.

Agronomic Applications

Translating computational discoveries into practical breeding applications through meta-QTL analysis and identification of genetic markers associated with stress tolerance and yield-related traits in economically important crops.

Research Objectives

  • Develop comprehensive genomic resources for understanding plant defense gene families and their evolutionary trajectories across major crop species
  • Build interpretable machine learning models that can predict gene function and regulatory networks from multi-omics data with high accuracy
  • Characterize stress-responsive transcriptional regulatory networks and identify key master regulators of plant defense pathways
  • Integrate computational predictions with experimental validation to establish functional relationships between genotype and phenotype
  • Create scalable bioinformatics workflows that can be applied to non-model crop species with limited genomic resources

Future Directions

Post-Doctoral Research Vision

During my post-doctoral training, I aim to develop scalable and interpretable AI models specifically designed for plant stress biology. My vision encompasses three major directions:

First, creating foundation models for plant genomics that can transfer knowledge across species and predict functional elements in newly sequenced genomes with minimal training data.

Second, developing explainable AI frameworks that not only achieve high predictive accuracy but also provide biological interpretability, enabling researchers to understand the underlying mechanisms driving model predictions.

Third, establishing integrative platforms that combine genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data to generate holistic models of plant stress responses that can guide precision breeding strategies.

Collaborative Approach

I believe that transformative advances in plant science emerge from collaborative efforts that bring together diverse expertise. Conclusion

As computational power continues to grow and sequencing costs decline, we stand at the threshold of a new era in plant biology where AI-driven approaches can dramatically accelerate our understanding of complex biological systems. My research program aims to harness these opportunities to address fundamental questions about plant defense mechanisms while simultaneously developing practical tools for crop improvement.
